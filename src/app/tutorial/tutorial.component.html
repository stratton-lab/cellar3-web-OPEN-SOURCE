Exploring Datasets

SingloCell makes it easy to browse and find datasets, whether you want to explore broadly or search for something specific.

The first way A is to use the search bar. Enter anything: dataset names, descriptions, species, tissues, keywords, category names, or experimental technologies (e.g., “MERFISH”) to instantly see matching datasets.
Query Example Explanation
injury Returns datasets containing the word "injury" in the name or description.
human Returns datasets from the human species.
James Watson Returns datasets where "James Watson" is listed as an author (defined in keywords).
MERFISH Returns datasets generated using the MERFISH technology.

The second way B is to explore datasets through a hierarchical category tree. You can browse step by step to discover available datasets, even if you're not exactly sure what you're looking for.

The third way C is to filter datasets using several powerful filters such as Modality, State, Species, Tissue, and more.

Interacting with the Main Plot

Once you select a dataset, you’ll land on its main page, where you’ll find all the essentials: the dataset’s name, tissue, species, total cell count, and quick links to related studies and GEO A. Front and center, you’ll see a large interactive plot displaying all the cells—arranged either by UMAP or, by their actual tissue locations for spatial datasets. You can zoom, pan, and play around with the plot to get a closer look F. Group or color the cells by condition, cell type, or sample to spot trends or differences in the data B. To view the expression levels of your genes of interest, simply click on the Gene Explorer C.

Gene Explorer

The Gene Explorer allows you to search for one or more genes of interest A, select them B, and visualize their expression across the dataset using UMAP C, Violin D, and Dot E plots.

Customizing Data

You can easily delete cells or reassign them to different clusters. First, select cells on the plot and click on "Analyze & Edit Selection" A. Then, click on the "Cleanup" tab in the dialog. From there, you'll have the option to reassign clusters B or delete cells C.

Analyzing Datasets in Real Time

Analysis What It Does How Results Are Visualized
Differential Gene Expression (DGE) Identifies genes with significant expression differences between cell groups. Interactive volcano plot; further exploration with violin, dot, and UMAP plots.
Functional Analysis Finds biological categories enriched in differentially expressed genes. Bar plots (hover for details on up/downregulated genes); network plots connect categories with shared genes.
KEGG Pathway Visualization Shows how significant genes interact in known biological pathways. KEGG diagrams highlight gene connections within pathways.
Pseudotime Trajectory Visualizes developmental progression of cells along a trajectory (if the data is available). Pseudotime plot with arrows/labels for direction and origin; gene expression can be overlaid in the same embedding.
Cell-Cell Interaction Explores communication between cell types through ligand-receptor interactions (if available). Chord diagram for interactions; heatmap for ligand-receptor pairs; supporting evidence with links or pathway diagrams.

Submitting Datasets

Step Description
1 Click the "SUBMIT DATASET" button in the top right corner of the website.
2 Fill out the pre-submission inquiry form.
3 Once we have reviewed your pre-submission inquiry, we will send you an email with a link to upload your RDS file.
4 Upload your RDS file. Please make sure it is under 2 GB (delete any unnecessary assays to reduce file size) and that gene symbols are used as gene names. All necessary QC and annotation steps should be completed prior to submission. Ensure that the expression data is normalized but not scaled.
5 Once we have performed QC on your dataset, you will receive a private link to access it.
